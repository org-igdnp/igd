As mentioned in the project document, organizations (district and village level councils) and institutions of indigenous peoples in the selected districts are the target groups of the project, and the individual Gurung, Tamang and Chepang and the Gurung, Tamang and Chepang communities in the project area whom the project directly works with,are the final beneficiaries of the project.

A total of 109,022 (F 47,443/M 61,579) people are the direct beneficiaries of the project, except the beneficiaries of Journal, calendar, documentary films telecasted via Sagarmatha Television and radio program. Of the total, 40,048 beneficiaries of (dialogue at target VDC, district and national level, informal class, competition, exposure, sharing interactions on NRM and between traditional medicine practitioner and health workers, startup workshop, mass based campaign, documentary shows, folk theatre and exhibition), 66,575 beneficiaries of (CPG, DCPG, IDP, MDT, CFP and PSC meeting), 1,903 beneficiaries organized into (CPG, DCPG, FTG, CC, IDP, MDT, CFP, PSC and producer's cooperatives), and 496 beneficiaries (ToT on cultural rights, cultural rights training, training to CCs, training on cultural dimensions, training to FTG, training on OMID, reinforcement workshop and skill enhancement training). 2.0 ASSESSMENT OF IMPLEMENTATION OF ACTION ACTIVITIES

2.1 Executive summary

(please give a global overview of the Action's implementation for the reporting period, no more than 1/2 page)

Nepal is a multi-cultural, multi-racial and multi-religious country. This fact has been recognized by the interim constitution promulgated following the termination of the decade long armed insurgency conducted by the Moaists. Needless to say, the Maoists have been political mainstreamed and they have emerged as the major players in the democratic polity of the country. Above one hundred indigenous ethnic groups inhabit in the country and out of them around 60 groups have been listed by the governemnt. At a time when cultural identities have been articulated and mobilized for political ends and their recognition and respect has been sought in the new federal constitution which is yet to be enacted, the project has worked for three years with major ethnic groups (the Gurungs, Tamangs, Chepangs) with a view to build their competence for enhancing cultural rights, conserving traditions and cultures and supporting them for livelihoods empowerment.

The project actions have followed the cascading approach to reach out to the target communities and a series of activities have been implemented to cater to this end. The key cultural and social representatives of the respective communitiies- the Gurungs, Tamanags and Chepangs have been trained and capacitated in the areas of both mobilization empowerment and livelihood empowerment. These crtical groups of people had been are mobilized to empower the target groups for building thweir awareness, enhancing their cultural rights and capacitate them for livelihood empowerment.

Furthermore, to leverage delivery and assure the effectiveness of the actions, the project has ensured the participation of respective partner IPOs from the beginning of project formulation and development stage, and accorded them a significant role and meaningful avenue to the partner IPOs in project implementation in their respective communities that helped to build ownership of the project among them. The project adopted the participatory process with a view in inculcating ownership of the target stakeholders in implementing the actions.
